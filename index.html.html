KERALA SADHYA

Have a look at Kerala's traditional dish!!

Sadhyais a feast of Kerala origin and of importance to all Malayalis, consisting of a variety of traditional vegetarian dishes usually served on a banana leaf in Kerala as lunch. Sadhya means banquet in Malayalam. Sadhya is typically served as a traditional dish for Onam, the state festival of Kerala.

ITEMS AND INGREDIENTS

  1. Upperi


    2. The one thing everyone loves the most - banana chips which is made of raw banana. A good handful is served at the very beginning followed by the other 25 dishes. Obviously by the time everything else arrives, you'll need another handful.


  2. Sharkara Varatti


    3. The sweeter version of banana chips. Made of jaggery, this hard to eat sweet will leave you wanting more.






  3. Manga Curry (Mango Curry)


    4. Plain old mango pickle to give your tongue a tangy taste of some amazing spices.






  4. Naranga Curry (Lemon Pickle)


    5. There will be two types of pickle in any onam sadhya for sure and this is the sour lemon pickle to add some zest to your taste buds.






  5. Erisseri


    6. A yummy dish made with pumpkin, red beans and a generous amount of grated coconut.






  6. Pulisseri


    7. Made with curd and your choice of vegetable ranging from pumpkin to cucumber and topped with a gracious amount of grated coconut.






  7. Kaalan

    8. A signature dish made with yogurt, yam or raw banana or of course our favourite, grated coconut.






  8. Olan

    9. Ash gourd and red beans splashed with a generous amount of hand-pressed, authentic coconut milk.






  9. Pachadi

    10. A delicious yogurt-based curry made with either pineapple or bitter gourd and, of course, grated coconut.






  10. Chena Mezhukkupuratti

    11. Yam cut into thin slices, boiled with spices and fried in coconut oil. You can't resist another helping of this.






  11. Inji Curry

    12. A must have for Onam, Inji curry is ginger, tamarind and jaggery. In most malayali households, this is the first thing that is prepared in the days leading to the 10th day of Onam.




  12. Parippu Curry

    13. Plain old moong dal topped with red chillies, black sesame seeds. Top it with ghee and can have a feeling of gastronomical heaven in your mouth.






  13. Chooru

    14. Well, this is just rice. But on Onam, most Malayalis serve red rice. If you haven't tasted this version of rice, well, you're missing out in life.






  14. Sambhar

    15. An essential dish of Sadhya, Sambhar is cooked with every vegetable there is from beans to potatoes to yam. Everything goes in to making this wonderful dish!.




  15. Pappadam

    16. Good old pappads made with rice flour. You'll need many of these to wholly enjoy your meal.






  16. Avial

    17. Mixed vegetables with grated coconut and made in coconut oil and milk. With so many dishes, this dish usually gets overlooked.One of the tasty mixed vegetable dish.




  17. Kichadi

    18. Spicy yogurt with your choice of vegetable. It can be okra, cucumber or even bitter gourd. Add it to your rice and parippu, and see how you lick your fingers in the end.




  18. Rasam

    19. Spicy tamarind soup, sprinkled with a generous amount of curry leaves, mustard and tomatoes. Drink it up in the end for digestion or just add it with your sambhar and rice.




  19. Koottu Curry

    20. Raw bananas and black chana with grated coconut made into a dry curry. You'll never even know it's raw banana, that's how well it is cooked.






  20. Neyy

    21. Popularly known as ghee, it serves a very important purpose. A spoonful of it on top of the rice and parippu, and you'll be licking your fingers.






  21. Inji Thayir

    22. Loads of thinly-sliced ginger, mixed with yogurt, black sesame seeds and spices. This one is absolutely lip-smacking!






  22. Mooru Kachiyadh

    23. Yogurt boiled to perfection with black sesame seeds, shallots, ginger and garlic.One of the healthy and delicious dish.






  23. Thoran

    24. A staple in every malayali household, thoran can be made from any vegetable. Usually, it's cabbage and carrots or just beans with grated coconut. Minimal cooking time and the perfect healthy dish to have with Mooru Kachiyadh.




  24. Poovan Pazham

    25. The smaller version of a banana, it adds taste to your payasam when mashed with hands and mixed well together. Don't forget to add a pappadam or two for that extra crunch.




  25. Palada Pradhaman

    26. The last dish that is served- a sweet dish made with milk, dry fruits and rice ada. The sweetness is just perfect to balance all the spices.






  26. Pazham Pradhaman

    27. Rice ada, cashew nuts, thinly sliced coconut pieces and jaggery. If you want to eat it the right way, refer to Poovan Pazham. You'll enjoy this delicacy much better.
